  1. SOFTGROUND, installation
    Happened at Columbus Park, Manhattan, on September 6-7, 2025, as part of People's Pavilion.

For the past four years I've been working at a bookstore in the Lower East Side. Whenever people ask where it is, I point to the New Museum as a reference. Yet just beside it stands a place with far longer history, one I rarely mention: the Bowery Mission, tucked beneath its scaffolding - its walkway occupied by pigeons or by the line of people waiting for meals and aid. The red door is always half-open, its interior withheld from view.

"

The line itself is a collage of faces and languages. When there aren't enough people to form a proper queue, those who have just received food sometimes break bread with the pigeons lingering nearby - a fragile moment of peace against the constant roar of Bowery's traffic. But proximity also brings unease. Those on their way to the Mission sometimes drift past the bookstore and step inside. Their behavior and clothing seem uniform, their purpose more direct, more stripped-down, and at times so random that it becomes harder to predict.

Whether their purpose truly conflicts with mine, or with the bookstore's, is not something I can know in advance. Still, tension surfaces all the same. My body reacts before thought, reading trust in a hem, a collar, a coat...and with it, to feel the gap stretch quietly, almost imperceptibly, yet further apart.

This project unfolds in a park, a public space, a surface without roof, a place where people wander, sometimes even live. It is the collection of infinite private spaces, and with the will to expand and blur our borders, we unsettle each other in uneasy peace. SOFT GROUND grows from this intention. It builds form from donations: three bubbles that swell and soften as they are filled with old belongings, a shared ground expanding through what is left behind.

Come by if you're available, at Columbus Park, Chinatown for People's Pavilion. Bring a few unwanted garments, or simply take a moment to sit and relax on the SOFT GROUND. All donations will go directly to the Bowery Mission."

31 pounds of recycled clothing collected on site were donated to the Bowery Mission a day after the event.
